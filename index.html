A ranked screening platform for ambient-pressure superconductivity Editorial preview — single issue
Untested candidates sit closest to verified anchors. Failure memory constrains where not to look.

This issue presents the first release of a structured screening platform for ambient-pressure high-temperature superconductivity, built around the bilayer nickelate La3Ni2O7 family. Every known superconductor, every published failure, and every candidate proposed by the platform's cascade screener is plotted on a single pressure–temperature landscape. Clicking a material opens its full catalog entry: measurement history, gate bitmask, nearest neighbors in feature space, and the ledger of how the platform's confidence in it evolved.

The work is assembled from peer-reviewed literature. The platform does not aggregate private laboratory data; its value is in reasoning carefully across what is already public. The goal is to help a researcher who is closer to the experimental frontier notice a candidate they had not considered. Contributions of new measurements, corrected failure notes, and synthesis recipe details are welcomed below.
