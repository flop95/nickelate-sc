Executive summary

La₃Ni₂O₇ bilayer nickelate superconductivity was discovered under high pressure in July 2023 (Tc ≈ 80K). Within 33 months, ambient-pressure superconductivity has been reported in epitaxially strained thin films, with onset Tc rising from 42K (Dec 2024) to 63K (Mar 2026). Bulk records under pressure have reached 96K in Sm-substituted bilayer crystals. The highest reported ambient onset Tc to date sits 14K below 77K — this is a one-sample gap and should not be read as a smooth approach to liquid-nitrogen ambient SC.

This brief presents a structured literature synthesis of published La₃Ni₂O₇ family measurements, with ambient thin films, films measured under applied pressure, and bulk-pressure anchors kept distinct. It identifies empirical patterns and research gaps, then lists five ranked, hypothesis-generating experiment prompts that, if the cited trends hold beyond the measured range, would test approaches to 77K at ambient pressure. Those prompts are empirical extrapolations, not predictions; the underlying linear-in-time progression is fit to a handful of anchor points and could change in either direction at any time.

Key patterns extracted from cross-referencing all data

Pattern 1SLAO dominates high-Tc ambient films. Ambient LAO, NGO, and STO films are present in the curated dataset, but every ambient film above 40K uses SrLaAlO₄. No substrate with a < 3.75Å has been tested (SmAlO₃ 3.734Å, EuAlO₃ 3.720Å remain untried).

Pattern 2Growth method is a leading variable in the current dataset. GAE extreme non-equilibrium growth produced 63K onset on the same substrate where PLD gives 42K. This is a large synthesis-associated effect in one same-substrate comparison and should be replicated before being treated as a general rule.

Pattern 3Oxygen vacancies occupy different sites in films vs. bulk. Compressive strain pushes vacancies from apical O (bulk) to planar NiO₂ sites (films), changing which oxygen sites are critical for SC optimization (Nat. Mater. 2025).

Pattern 4Superconducting dome resembles cuprate phenomenology. Sr-doping maps a dome (x = 0 to 0.38) with a Hall sign change near optimal doping, echoing electron-doped cuprate phenomenology without proving the same mechanism (PRL 136, Feb 2026).

Pattern 5γII Fermi pocket — single-paper, hypothesis-grade. One Nature paper (Apr 9, 2026) reports the γII pocket present in SC superstructures (1212, 2222, 2323) and absent in non-SC 1313. Read this as a candidate screening idea from a single figure in a single paper, not a validated criterion. Pockets-as-SC-fingerprints have historically failed to generalize; the original figures need cross-checking before reuse, and independent confirmation is required before the pattern should propagate.

Pattern 6Strange-metal behavior tracks Tc in this small set. Highest-Tc films (63K) show resistivity ∝ T¹ (α ≈ 1), while lower-Tc films show Fermi-liquid T² dependence. The association is plausible but small-n.

Pattern 7c/a ratio is a leading empirical parameter within the measured series. In the curated substrate/pressure series, onset Tc increases with c/a (Comm. Phys. 2025). c/a encodes both strain and orbital splitting effects; extrapolation outside the measured c/a range is the risky step.

Research gaps — untested combinations

Gap 1No films on substrates with a < 3.75Å. Stronger compression could push c/a higher and increase Tc — or cause film decoherence. This is the most obvious untested axis.

Gap 2Sr-doping dome only mapped on SLAO. Does optimal doping shift on different substrates? Zero data exists.

Gap 3Electric field gating of single-bilayer films proposed theoretically in one Nat. Commun. (2026) paper; the cited 77K+ figure is a model-dependent upper bound, conditioned on intralayer d-wave pairing. Zero experimental attempts published.

Gap 4Pressure applied to GAE-grown 63K films not yet reported. Lower-quality films already show 62→68.5K at 2 GPa (arXiv:2603.29531† — identifier flagged for verification, see dataset note).

Gap 5No lanthanide sweep beyond Pr substitution. Nd, Sm, Gd on La site untested in films.

Gap 6Pairing symmetry in films unconfirmed experimentally — s± vs d-wave has direct implications for optimization strategy.

Ranked experiment prompts

These are empirical, hypothesis-generating experiment rankings derived from the curated measurements and cited trend claims. Labels describe heuristic priority/proximity, not probability. They are not first-principles simulations and are not verified outcomes.

P1 Near-term test
Apply 2–5 GPa pressure to GAE-grown 63K (La,Pr)₃Ni₂O₇ films.

Films in the cited pressure series report 62→68.5K at 2 GPa (Zhao/Sun 2026). If GAE films with fewer oxygen vacancies follow the same empirical pressure trend, a hypothesis-generating scenario range is 75–85K. Achievable with piston-cylinder pressure cells, not diamond anvils, but still unverified for the 63K GAE films.

P2 Extrapolative test
GAE-grown (La,Pr)₃Ni₂O₇ on SmAlO₃ (a = 3.734Å, strain ≈ -2.6%).

If the c/a trend holds beyond the measured range, extra compression could increase onset Tc by 10–15K. Key risk: coherent film growth at higher mismatch. No data exists for this substrate.

P3 Combination test
Optimal Sr-doping (x ≈ 0.15) combined with GAE growth on SLAO.

The dome study used MBE, not GAE. Combining the best doping level with the best growth method has not been attempted. Directly targets the zero-R Tc gap.

P4 Theory-led test
Perpendicular electric field gating of single-bilayer films (0.1–0.2V).

A single theory paper (Nat. Commun. 2026) argues that perpendicular gating could enhance intralayer d-wave pairing; the 77K+ figure is its theoretical upper bound, conditioned on the assumption that the pairing channel is intralayer d-wave. Zero experimental attempts exist in the curated dataset, and the pairing symmetry in films is itself unconfirmed (see Gap 6). Treat the 77K+ number as a model-dependent target, not a prediction. Any group with ionic-liquid gating capability could begin testing whether the gating direction matters at all.

P5 Exploratory
Engineer superstructures on substrates with a < 3.75Å.

The γII pocket is reported as an SC-associated signature (Nature, Apr 9 2026). Test whether stronger strain preserves or destroys this pocket while pushing Tc higher. Requires ARPES + film growth capability.

Rate of progress and projections

From first ambient film (42K, Dec 2024) to current record (63K, Mar 2026): +21K in 15 months. A naive linear fit through this would cross 77K by mid-2027. The fit is across only three or four anchor points; two of those (42K and 63K) sit on the same SLAO substrate and reflect different growth methods (PLD vs GAE) rather than improvement of a single method, so this is not really a "rate" in the conventional sense. Treat it as a back-of-envelope reference number for follow-up planning, not a forecast. Any one of substrate, growth method, doping, or applied field could change the trajectory in either direction.

The film + moderate pressure route is a near-term hypothesis: current pressure-series films (not the 63K GAE film) reach 68.5K at 2 GPa. If the observed ~3K/GPa sensitivity continued, a 5 GPa point would land near ~77K. This is testable with clamp pressure cells, but it remains an unverified extrapolation; the GAE-grown 63K films may show a different pressure response.

Across these material families, a recurring strategy appears: compress the lattice into a high-Tc configuration, then use a physical trick (quenching, strain, interface coupling) to retain or mimic that configuration at lower pressure. This convergence is used here as a heuristic for hypothesis generation, not as a settled mechanism. The Hg-1223 pressure-quench row is a source-verification checkpoint, not a load-bearing premise for citing this brief.

About this analysis

This research brief was assembled through extraction and cross-referencing of published experimental data from peer-reviewed journals and preprints (Nature, Nature Materials, Nature Physics, Nature Communications, Physical Review Letters, Communications Physics, National Science Review, and arXiv). All 23 curated records trace to specific publications cited in the dataset. Hypothesis-candidate rows are empirical trend extrapolations, not first-principles simulations or forecasts, and should be treated as experimental follow-up prompts. The interactive screening tools (substrate screener, data engine, timeline tracker) are available as companion artifacts.

Corrections and new measurements are reviewed before inclusion via GitHub issues. Source verification is required. The pseudonymous authorship, small corpus, and heuristic ranking method should be visible in any reuse of this material; cite the Zenodo DOI for the version you used.
