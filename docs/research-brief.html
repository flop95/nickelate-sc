Executive summary

La₃Ni₂O₇ bilayer nickelate superconductivity was discovered under high pressure in July 2023 (Tc ≈ 80K). Within 33 months, ambient-pressure superconductivity has been achieved in epitaxially strained thin films, with onset Tc rising from 42K (Dec 2024) to 63K (Mar 2026). Bulk records under pressure have reached 96K. The gap between the ambient film record and the liquid nitrogen threshold (77K) is now only 14K.

This brief presents a structured meta-analysis of all published La₃Ni₂O₇ family thin film results, identifies systematic patterns and research gaps, and generates five ranked, testable predictions for crossing the 77K threshold at ambient pressure. No comparable structured dataset exists in the published literature as of April 2026.

Key patterns extracted from cross-referencing all data

PATTERN 1 Only one substrate explored at ambient. Every ambient-pressure SC film uses SrLaAlO₄. No substrates with a < 3.75Å have been tested (SmAlO₃ 3.734Å, EuAlO₃ 3.720Å remain untried).

PATTERN 2 Growth method dominates over composition. GAE extreme non-equilibrium growth produced 63K onset on the same substrate where PLD gives 42K — a 50% increase from synthesis optimization alone.

PATTERN 3 Oxygen vacancies occupy different sites in films vs. bulk. Compressive strain pushes vacancies from apical O (bulk) to planar NiO₂ sites (films), changing which oxygen sites are critical for SC optimization (Nat. Mater. 2025).

PATTERN 4 Superconducting dome parallels cuprates. Sr-doping maps a dome (x = 0 to 0.38) with Hall sign change at optimal doping — identical to electron-doped cuprate phenomenology (PRL 136, Feb 2026).

PATTERN 5 γII Fermi pocket is the topological SC signature. Present in all SC superstructures (1212, 2222, 2323), absent in non-SC 1313 — the first structural screening criterion for nickelate SC (Nature, Apr 9 2026).

PATTERN 6 Strange-metal behavior tracks Tc. Highest-Tc films (63K) show resistivity ∝ T¹ (α ≈ 1), while lower-Tc films show Fermi-liquid T² dependence. SC strength is linked to non-Fermi-liquid normal state.

PATTERN 7 c/a ratio is the master parameter. Across all substrates and pressures, onset Tc increases monotonically with c/a (Comm. Phys. 2025). c/a encodes both strain and orbital splitting effects.

Research gaps — untested combinations

GAP 1 No films on substrates with a < 3.75Å. Stronger compression could push c/a higher and increase Tc — or cause film decoherence. This is the most obvious untested axis.

GAP 2 Sr-doping dome only mapped on SLAO. Does optimal doping shift on different substrates? Zero data exists.

GAP 3 Electric field gating of single-bilayer films proposed theoretically (Nat. Commun. Jan 2026, predicts 77K+). Zero experimental attempts published.

GAP 4 Pressure applied to GAE-grown 63K films not yet reported. Lower-quality films already show 62→68.5K at 2 GPa (arXiv:2603.29531).

GAP 5 No lanthanide sweep beyond Pr substitution. Nd, Sm, Gd on La site untested in films.

GAP 6 Pairing symmetry in films unconfirmed experimentally — s± vs d-wave has direct implications for optimization strategy.

Ranked predictions

P1 — HIGH CONFIDENCE

Apply 2-5 GPa pressure to GAE-grown 63K (La,Pr)₃Ni₂O₇ films. Lower-quality films jump 62→68.5K at 2 GPa (Zhao/Sun 2026). GAE films with fewer oxygen vacancies should respond more strongly. Predicted onset: 75-85K. Achievable with piston-cylinder pressure cells, not diamond anvils.

P2 — MEDIUM-HIGH CONFIDENCE

GAE-grown (La,Pr)₃Ni₂O₇ on SmAlO₃ (a = 3.734Å, strain ≈ -2.6%). If the c/a monotonic trend holds, extra compression should increase onset Tc by 10-15K. Key risk: coherent film growth at higher mismatch. No data exists for this substrate.

P3 — MEDIUM CONFIDENCE

Optimal Sr-doping (x ≈ 0.15) combined with GAE growth on SLAO. The dome study used MBE, not GAE. Combining the best doping level with the best growth method has not been attempted. Directly targets the zero-R Tc gap.

P4 — MEDIUM CONFIDENCE

Perpendicular electric field gating of single-bilayer films (0.1-0.2V). Theoretical prediction published in Nat. Commun. (2026): intralayer d-wave pairing enhanced to 77K+. Zero experimental attempts. Any group with ionic liquid gating capability could try this.

P5 — EXPLORATORY

Engineer superstructures on substrates with a < 3.75Å. The γII pocket is confirmed as the SC signature (Nature, Apr 9 2026). Test whether stronger strain preserves or destroys this pocket while pushing Tc higher. Requires ARPES + film growth capability.

Rate of progress and projections

From first ambient film (42K, Dec 2024) to current record (63K, Mar 2026): +21K in 15 months. At this rate, ambient films cross 77K by mid-2027. The rate is likely to accelerate as the GAE method is adopted by more groups and untested parameter space (substrates, doping, electric fields) is explored.

The film + moderate pressure route is the fastest path: current films at 68.5K with just 2 GPa. At ~3K/GPa sensitivity, 5 GPa would reach ~77K. This is achievable with clamp pressure cells that cost under $10,000.

Three independent material families discovered the same strategy: compress the lattice into a high-Tc configuration, then use a physical trick (quenching, strain, interface coupling) to lock that structure at ambient pressure. This convergence suggests a general principle that may apply to future material systems.

About this analysis

This research brief was generated through systematic extraction and cross-referencing of published experimental data from peer-reviewed journals and preprints (Nature, Nature Materials, Nature Physics, Nature Communications, Physical Review Letters, Communications Physics, National Science Review, and arXiv). All 18 data points trace to specific publications cited in the interactive dataset. Predictions are based on empirical trend extrapolation, not first-principles simulation, and should be treated as hypothesis generation for experimental follow-up. The interactive screening tools (substrate screener, data engine, timeline tracker) are available as companion artifacts.
