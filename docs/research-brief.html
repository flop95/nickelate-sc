Executive summary

La₃Ni₂O₇ bilayer nickelate superconductivity was discovered under high pressure in July 2023 (Tc ≈ 80K). Within 33 months, ambient-pressure superconductivity has been achieved in epitaxially strained thin films, with onset Tc rising from 42K (Dec 2024) to 63K (Mar 2026). Bulk records under pressure have reached 96K in Sm-substituted bilayer crystals. The gap between the ambient film record and the liquid nitrogen threshold (77K) is now only 14K.

This brief presents a structured literature synthesis of published La₃Ni₂O₇ family measurements, with ambient thin films, films measured under applied pressure, and bulk-pressure anchors kept distinct. It identifies empirical patterns and research gaps, then lists five ranked, testable hypotheses for crossing the 77K threshold at ambient pressure. Those hypotheses are empirical extrapolations, not verified published measurements.

Key patterns extracted from cross-referencing all data

PATTERN 1 SLAO dominates high-Tc ambient films. Ambient LAO, NGO, and STO films are present in the curated dataset, but every ambient film above 40K uses SrLaAlO₄. No substrate with a < 3.75Å has been tested (SmAlO₃ 3.734Å, EuAlO₃ 3.720Å remain untried).

PATTERN 2 Growth method is a leading variable in the current dataset. GAE extreme non-equilibrium growth produced 63K onset on the same substrate where PLD gives 42K. This is a large synthesis-associated effect in one same-substrate comparison and should be replicated before being treated as a general rule.

PATTERN 3 Oxygen vacancies occupy different sites in films vs. bulk. Compressive strain pushes vacancies from apical O (bulk) to planar NiO₂ sites (films), changing which oxygen sites are critical for SC optimization (Nat. Mater. 2025).

PATTERN 4 Superconducting dome resembles cuprate phenomenology. Sr-doping maps a dome (x = 0 to 0.38) with a Hall sign change near optimal doping, echoing electron-doped cuprate phenomenology without proving the same mechanism (PRL 136, Feb 2026).

PATTERN 5 γII Fermi pocket is a reported SC-associated signature. Present in reported SC superstructures (1212, 2222, 2323), absent in non-SC 1313 — a candidate structural screening criterion that should be checked against the original figures before reuse (Nature, Apr 9 2026).

PATTERN 6 Strange-metal behavior tracks Tc in this small set. Highest-Tc films (63K) show resistivity ∝ T¹ (α ≈ 1), while lower-Tc films show Fermi-liquid T² dependence. The association is plausible but small-n.

PATTERN 7 c/a ratio is a leading empirical parameter within the measured series. In the curated substrate/pressure series, onset Tc increases with c/a (Comm. Phys. 2025). c/a encodes both strain and orbital splitting effects; extrapolation outside the measured c/a range is the risky step.

Research gaps — untested combinations

GAP 1 No films on substrates with a < 3.75Å. Stronger compression could push c/a higher and increase Tc — or cause film decoherence. This is the most obvious untested axis.

GAP 2 Sr-doping dome only mapped on SLAO. Does optimal doping shift on different substrates? Zero data exists.

GAP 3 Electric field gating of single-bilayer films proposed theoretically (Nat. Commun. Jan 2026, predicts 77K+). Zero experimental attempts published.

GAP 4 Pressure applied to GAE-grown 63K films not yet reported. Lower-quality films already show 62→68.5K at 2 GPa (arXiv:2603.29531).

GAP 5 No lanthanide sweep beyond Pr substitution. Nd, Sm, Gd on La site untested in films.

GAP 6 Pairing symmetry in films unconfirmed experimentally — s± vs d-wave has direct implications for optimization strategy.

Ranked experiment prompts

These are empirical, hypothesis-generating experiment rankings derived from the curated measurements and cited trend claims. Labels describe heuristic priority/proximity, not probability. They are not first-principles simulations and are not verified outcomes.

P1 — NEAR-TERM TEST

Apply 2-5 GPa pressure to GAE-grown 63K (La,Pr)₃Ni₂O₇ films. Films in the cited pressure series report 62→68.5K at 2 GPa (Zhao/Sun 2026). If GAE films with fewer oxygen vacancies follow the same empirical pressure trend, a hypothesis-generating scenario range is 75-85K. Achievable with piston-cylinder pressure cells, not diamond anvils, but still unverified for the 63K GAE films.

P2 — EXTRAPOLATIVE TEST

GAE-grown (La,Pr)₃Ni₂O₇ on SmAlO₃ (a = 3.734Å, strain ≈ -2.6%). If the c/a trend holds beyond the measured range, extra compression could increase onset Tc by 10-15K. Key risk: coherent film growth at higher mismatch. No data exists for this substrate.

P3 — COMBINATION TEST

Optimal Sr-doping (x ≈ 0.15) combined with GAE growth on SLAO. The dome study used MBE, not GAE. Combining the best doping level with the best growth method has not been attempted. Directly targets the zero-R Tc gap.

P4 — THEORY-LED TEST

Perpendicular electric field gating of single-bilayer films (0.1-0.2V). Theory paper published in Nat. Commun. (2026): intralayer d-wave pairing enhanced to 77K+. Zero experimental attempts in the curated dataset. Any group with ionic liquid gating capability could test the claim.

P5 — EXPLORATORY

Engineer superstructures on substrates with a < 3.75Å. The γII pocket is reported as an SC-associated signature (Nature, Apr 9 2026). Test whether stronger strain preserves or destroys this pocket while pushing Tc higher. Requires ARPES + film growth capability.

Rate of progress and projections

From first ambient film (42K, Dec 2024) to current record (63K, Mar 2026): +21K in 15 months. If that empirical rate continued unchanged, ambient films would cross 77K by mid-2027. This is a projection, not a guarantee; adoption of GAE and exploration of substrates, doping, and electric fields could change the slope in either direction.

The film + moderate pressure route is a near-term hypothesis: current films reach 68.5K at 2 GPa. Extrapolating the observed ~3K/GPa sensitivity, 5 GPa would land near ~77K. This is testable with clamp pressure cells, but it remains an unverified extrapolation for GAE-grown 63K films.

Across these material families, a recurring strategy appears: compress the lattice into a high-Tc configuration, then use a physical trick (quenching, strain, interface coupling) to retain or mimic that configuration at lower pressure. This convergence is used here as a heuristic for hypothesis generation, not as a settled mechanism. About this analysis

This research brief was generated through extraction and cross-referencing of published experimental data from peer-reviewed journals and preprints (Nature, Nature Materials, Nature Physics, Nature Communications, Physical Review Letters, Communications Physics, National Science Review, and arXiv). All 23 curated records trace to specific publications cited in the dataset. Predictions are based on empirical trend extrapolation, not first-principles simulation, and should be treated as hypothesis generation for experimental follow-up. The interactive screening tools (substrate screener, data engine, timeline tracker) are available as companion artifacts.

Corrections and new measurements are reviewed before inclusion. Source verification is required. The pseudonymous authorship, private source repository, small corpus, and heuristic ranking method should be visible in any reuse of this material.
